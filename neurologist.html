Symptoms Disease Recommended Medicine
Memory loss, confusion, difficulty with speech and decision-making, mood changes. Alzheimer Donepezil, Rivastigmine, Galantamine (cholinesterase inhibitors), Memantine (NMDA antagonist).
Seizures (generalized, focal), loss of consciousness, muscle spasms, confusion after seizures. Epilepsy Valproic acid, Lamotrigine, Phenytoin, Levetiracetam.
Headaches, nausea, vomiting, vision problems, seizures, cognitive changes. Brain Tumor Dexamethasone (steroid for swelling), Temozolomide (chemotherapy), Anticonvulsants (for seizures).
Severe, throbbing headache, nausea, sensitivity to light/sound, aura (visual disturbances). Migraine Sumatriptan, Ibuprofen, Ergotamine, Beta-blockers (for prevention), Antiemetics (for nausea).
Muscle weakness, difficulty speaking/swallowing, spasticity, paralysis. Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS) Riluzole, Edaravone (helps slow progression).
Memory loss, confusion, difficulty concentrating, changes in mood, personality. Dementia Donepezil, Rivastigmine, Galantamine, Memantine.
Tremors, rigidity, bradykinesia (slowness of movement), balance issues. Parkinson's Disease Levodopa, Carbidopa, Dopamine agonists (Pramipexole), MAO-B inhibitors (Selegiline).
Involuntary movements (chorea), mood changes, difficulty with speech and swallowing, cognitive decline. Huntington's Disease Tetrabenazine (for chorea), Antidepressants (SSRIs), Antipsychotics.
Numbness, vision problems, muscle weakness, difficulty with coordination, fatigue. Multiple Sclerosis (MS) Disease-modifying therapies (e.g., Interferon beta, Glatiramer acetate), Corticosteroids for flare-ups.
Varies by condition, but may include memory loss, tremors, motor difficulties, and cognitive decline. Neurodegenerative Disease Disease-specific drugs like those listed for Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, etc.
Sudden weakness/numbness (especially on one side), speech difficulty, vision changes, confusion. Stroke Thrombolytics (e.g., Alteplase for ischemic stroke), Antiplatelets (e.g., Aspirin), Anticoagulants.
Headache, dizziness, memory loss, nausea, changes in mood, loss of consciousness. Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI) Pain relievers (e.g., Acetaminophen, Ibuprofen), Anticonvulsants (for seizures), Antidepressants.
Headache, fever, stiff neck, nausea, sensitivity to light, confusion. Meningitis Antibiotics (for bacterial meningitis), Antivirals (for viral meningitis), Corticosteroids.
Hallucinations, delusions, disorganized speech, lack of motivation, impaired functioning. Schizophrenia Antipsychotics (e.g., Olanzapine, Risperidone, Clozapine).
Involuntary tics (motor and vocal), blinking, head jerking, vocalizations. Tourette Syndrome Tetrabenazine, Clonidine, Antipsychotics (e.g., Aripiprazole).
Difficulty speaking, understanding speech, reading, and writing due to brain damage (often from stroke). Aphasia No specific medications, but speech therapy and language rehabilitation are key.
Sudden, temporary facial paralysis, weakness on one side of the face. Bell's Palsy Corticosteroids (e.g., Prednisone), Antiviral medications (if a viral cause is suspected).
Severe headache, nausea, vision problems, neck pain, sudden loss of consciousness. Brain Aneurysm Antihypertensives (to lower blood pressure), Pain relievers, Anticonvulsants (for seizures)
Muscle tightness, spasms, difficulty walking, balance problems, delayed motor skills. Cerebral Palsy Baclofen (muscle relaxant), Botox (for spasticity), Anticonvulsants (for seizures).
Weakness, speech difficulty, vision changes, sudden headache (stroke-like symptoms Cerebrovascular Disease Antiplatelets (e.g., Aspirin), Anticoagulants, Statins (for cholesterol control).
Headache, dizziness, nausea, confusion, sensitivity to light and noise. Concussion Pain relievers (e.g., Acetaminophen, Ibuprofen), Antiemetics (for nausea), No specific treatment for the concussion itself.
Persistent sadness, loss of interest, fatigue, changes in appetite/sleep, feelings of worthlessness. Depression Antidepressants (e.g., SSRIs, SNRIs like Sertraline, Venlafaxine), Therapy (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy).
